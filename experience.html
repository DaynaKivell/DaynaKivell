Technical Innovation & Prototyping

Driving innovation through prototyping and rapid iteration has been a defining aspect of my career. I thrive on taking ideas from concept to reality, ensuring they meet customer and market demands.

From Concept to Prototype: Retrofit Insulation Robot

Situation: To improve home warmth and energy efficiency, we set out to create a robot capable of navigating under suspended floors to spray insulation on their underside. This solution had to be cost-effective, reliable, and user-friendly.

Actions: We designed and iterated multiple prototypes, refining the mechanics and sensor selection to balance budget constraints with performance. We worked closely with end users to understand operational challenges and ensure usability. Strategic testing allowed us to optimise control systems, mobility, and insulation application.

Result: The final product is now successfully deployed, delivering effective insulation with a robust and efficient design, improving home energy efficiency - 5000 installs across the UK and a small number in the EU.

Proving Product Effectiveness with Data Logging

Situation: Demonstrating the effectiveness of our insulation solution required quantifiable proof of energy savings and temperature improvement.

Actions: We first conducted controlled testing in a mock house, installing data loggers to measure temperature distribution and energy usage before and after insulation application. Following successful initial trials, we expanded the analysis to real homes, gathering data on heating cost reductions and overall comfort improvements.

Result: The analysis showed a significant improvement in temperature consistency, reducing the difference from head to toe from ~5°C to ±1-2°C. The findings validated the system’s impact on home warmth, energy efficiency, and cost savings.

Iterating for Continuous Improvement

Situation: With technological advancements, maintaining product competitiveness required continuous iteration and refinement.

Actions: We systematically reviewed emerging technologies to enhance user experience and efficiency. Improvements included refining mobility, optimising insulation dispersion, and integrating new sensor capabilities to enhance precision.

Result: Each iteration enhanced reliability, ease of use, and operational efficiency, ensuring the product remained at the forefront of retrofit insulation technology.

Engineering Solutions for Efficiency

Situation: Early iterations of our robotic system relied on multiple off-the-shelf components—separate power supplies, network routers, and laptops—which added complexity and inefficiencies.

Actions: We engineered a streamlined control unit, integrating power supply, mini-computer, and network switch into a single, compact module. This reduced setup complexity, increased system reliability, and improved portability.

Result: The redesigned system simplified deployment, reduced costs, and enhanced operational efficiency, making the technology more accessible and scalable. We applied this same thinking to the internals of the robots, the spray gun, and the handle.

Engineering Intelligence & Operational Strategy

Leveraging data to refine operations, improve reliability, and enhance efficiency is at the core of my work. I specialise in identifying patterns, developing targeted solutions, and ensuring seamless integration of new processes to optimise performance.

Transforming Robot Maintenance with Data-Driven Insights

Situation: Our robots operate in harsh environments, leading to frequent breakdowns. We needed to pinpoint the main failure points and develop solutions to improve fleet reliability.

Actions: My team analysed fault reports and identified that:

  • 48% of failures could be resolved through improved installer training.
  • 33% could be mitigated by upskilling specific installers as Robot Gurus—on-site specialists equipped to diagnose and repair issues using custom-built test jigs and stocked spare parts.
  • 19% were catastrophic failures caused by chemical leaks or dirt ingress. We worked with the development team to design and roll out a robot sealing procedure to prevent contamination.

Result: After implementing these initiatives, we saw a significant reduction in robot failures. Sealed robots were able to operate in extreme conditions without internal damage, reducing downtime and repair costs.

Scaling Knowledge: Training for Operational Efficiency

Situation: With a growing installer base, we needed a scalable way to ensure consistent training, reduce faults, and improve troubleshooting capabilities across teams.

Actions: We developed a structured training programme that included:

  • Online modules for onboarding and refresher courses.
  • Hands-on workshops for base-level installers and advanced training for Robot Gurus.
  • Annual refresher courses to keep skills sharp.

Result: The programme standardised best practices across teams, reducing avoidable robot failures and increasing overall operational efficiency.

Empowering Field Teams: Service & Maintenance App

Situation: Installers needed a more efficient way to diagnose and resolve robot faults on-site, reducing reliance on remote support and minimising downtime.

Actions: My team has been developing a service & maintenance app designed to:

  • Guide installers through fault diagnosis step by step.
  • Provide instant access to troubleshooting procedures and repair documentation.
  • Streamline part requests and service logs directly from the control unit.

Result: Initial testing has demonstrated a clear reduction in troubleshooting time, with installers able to resolve common issues independently. The app continues to evolve based on user feedback, ensuring it meets the needs of field teams effectively.

Optimising Inventory: Stock & Spares Management

Situation: Tracking spare parts usage was a challenge, leading to stock shortages and inefficiencies.

Actions: My team implemented a stock & spares tracking system for our mini warehouse, enabling us to:

  • Monitor parts usage in real time.
  • Proactively order replacements.
  • Identify trends in component failures to inform future robot design improvements.

Result: We optimised stock levels, reduced downtime due to missing parts, and fed insights back into the development team to drive design enhancements.

Final Refinements & Next Steps

At every stage, we feed data back into the development team. We ask:

  • Can we retrofit a solution to current robots to improve reliability and reduce costs?
  • Is this a fundamental issue that needs to be designed out in the next generation?

This iterative feedback loop ensures our robots are always improving, becoming more robust, cost-effective, and user-friendly.
